# Vouched
### *The manual nobody gave you on day one.*

---

Some students arrive at college already knowing the game.
They know which professors actually write strong recommendations — and which ones just say yes.
They know the scholarship portal crashes on the last day, so you submit a week early.
They know the top placement club stopped reading applications after the first 50.

They know because someone told them. A parent. A cousin. A family friend who went to the same college.

First-generation students don't get that conversation.

**Vouched exists to give it to them.**

---

## What Vouched Is

Vouched is a college-specific intelligence platform built on a single idea:
**seniors who've lived it, sharing what they know — with their reputation on the line.**

This isn't a notice board. It isn't a WhatsApp group with better UI.
Every tip on Vouched is backed by a real person whose credibility score moves with every piece of advice they give. If they're right, they build trust. If they're wrong, they lose it.

That one mechanic changes everything.

---

## The Features

### 🔍 Intelligence Feed
Not chronological. Not algorithmic. Ranked by **urgency × contributor credibility** — so the most important, most trustworthy tips surface first. Every tip is tagged by college, branch, year, and category. When a tip's window closes, it disappears. No clutter. No stale advice.

### ✍️ Tip Submission
Seniors don't just post — they commit. A structured form asks: what's the tip, what's the window, what happens if you miss it, and how do you know. Every tip needs a co-signer before it goes live. Skin in the game, from the start.

### 📊 Credibility Score Engine
Public. Permanent. College-specific. Your score rises when your tips prove accurate. It falls when disputes are upheld against you. It's the only number that matters on this platform — and everyone can see it.

### ⚖️ Friction-Based Dispute System
Reporting a tip isn't a button tap. It's a structured process — what was wrong, how you verified it, what actually happened. Disputes go to a panel of three high-credibility seniors who review independently. If your dispute holds, the tip comes down and the contributor loses credibility. If it doesn't, you do. No room for bad-faith reports.

### 🔔 Proactive Nudge Engine
Set your profile once — college, branch, year, interests. The platform maps every relevant tip to your academic calendar and reaches you at **3 weeks, 1 week, and 3 days** before the window closes. Not just a date. The full context. The thing you actually need to know.

### 📖 Unwritten Rules Archive
The permanent, living record of how your college actually works. Anonymous submissions, editorial moderation. A rotating panel of trusted seniors filters every entry before it publishes. Personal attacks don't make it through. Systemic truths do. Entries expire after two academic years unless a current senior re-vouches them.

### 🔒 Trust Circles
Private groups. Maximum 20 people. Invite only. For the tips too sensitive to post publicly — the ones that need a trusted room. If enough circle members vouch a tip, it can be anonymously escalated to the public feed.

### 🎓 Senior Exit Debrief
Before seniors leave, they answer 10 structured questions: best decisions, biggest misses, what they'd tell their first-year self. It gets indexed into the archive permanently. It earns them a **Legacy Badge**. It's the closest thing to a passing of the torch this platform has.

---

## The Moderation Line

One rule. Applied consistently.

> **Systemic observations are allowed. Personal attacks are not.**

| ✅ Allowed | ❌ Not Allowed |
|---|---|
| "This prof rarely gives A grades regardless of work quality" | "This prof is an idiot" |
| "Club X has a backdoor for students from a specific hostel" | "The club president is corrupt" |
| "Department admin loses paperwork — follow up at least 3 times" | Naming and shaming individuals |

## Why It Works

Vouched doesn't ask anyone to be altruistic.

It gives seniors a reason to be **accurate** — their reputation.
It gives freshers a reason to **trust** — the credibility score.
It gives moderators a reason to **participate** — credibility points.

Every incentive on this platform points toward truth.
